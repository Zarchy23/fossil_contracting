Harare–Chirundu Road Rehabilitation Project

Zimbabwe's Major National Infrastructure Development

The Harare–Chirundu Road Rehabilitation Project is one of Zimbabwe's major national infrastructure developments aimed at improving transportation efficiency, road safety, and regional trade connectivity. Fossil Contracting is actively involved in the widening and rehabilitation of the highway, which serves as a key trade route linking Zimbabwe to Zambia and other regional markets.

The project involves road widening, asphalt surfacing, drainage systems, earthworks, road rehabilitation, and infrastructure modernization to improve traffic flow and reduce travel time. With a project value of approximately USD 365 million, the development demonstrates Fossil Contracting's capability in delivering large-scale, high-impact civil engineering projects that support economic growth and national development.

Project Details

Location: Harare to Chirundu, Zimbabwe

Client: Ministry of Transport

Status: Completed

Completion: 100%

Duration: January 2024 - December 2025

Project Scope

  • Road widening
  • Asphalt surfacing
  • Drainage systems
  • Earthworks
  • Road rehabilitation
  • Infrastructure modernization
  • Improved traffic flow
