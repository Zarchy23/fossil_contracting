Harare–Beitbridge Road Rehabilitation Project

Strategic Regional Infrastructure Development - Zimbabwe

← Back to Projects

The Harare–Beitbridge Road Rehabilitation Project is one of Zimbabwe's flagship national infrastructure developments aimed at modernizing the country's major transport corridor connecting Harare to Beitbridge Border Post and the Southern African region. Fossil Contracting is among the key contractors participating in the rehabilitation and widening of the highway to improve transportation efficiency, road safety, and regional trade connectivity.

The highway is a strategic regional trade route that facilitates the movement of commercial goods, mining products, fuel, and passenger traffic between Zimbabwe and neighboring countries, particularly South Africa. The rehabilitation project is therefore essential in supporting economic growth, improving logistics efficiency, and reducing transport delays.

The project demonstrates Fossil Contracting's expertise in handling large-scale infrastructure developments using modern construction technologies, experienced engineering teams, and advanced heavy-duty equipment. Through this initiative, Fossil Contracting continues to play a major role in Zimbabwe's infrastructure transformation, delivering durable, high-quality road networks that improve connectivity, support industrial growth, and strengthen regional economic integration.

Project Details

Location: Harare to Beitbridge, Zimbabwe

Client: City Council

Status: ONGOING

Completion: 75%

Duration: 2024 - Ongoing

Project Scope

  • Road widening and full rehabilitation
  • Asphalt surfacing and pavement strengthening
  • Earthworks and excavation operations
  • Construction and rehabilitation of bridges and culverts
  • Drainage and stormwater management systems
  • Road signage and safety improvements
  • Installation of modern transport infrastructure
  • Regional trade route modernization
